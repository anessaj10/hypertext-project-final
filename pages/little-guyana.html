richmond hill, queens

🗽 little Guyana

home is not a place.
it's the people and the plates.

Growing up in Richmond Hill, Queens,
the smells, the sounds, the vibes and roti shops —
i felt like my roots were always with me.

where to eat —

Sybil's Bakery & Restaurant Liberty Ave & 133rd St — the cornerstone of Little Guyana. open since 1976. always a line. but always worth it. a few of my faves include the fish sandwich, bake & saltfish, and pine tarts.
Kaieteur Express Liberty Ave & 120th St — Guyanese, West Indian, and Chinese comfort food all under one roof. huge portions and yummy pepper sauce.
Little Guyana Bake Shop Liberty Ave — tennis rolls, black eye cakes, and plait bread. the kind of place that feels like someone's kitchen.
New Thriving Restaurant Liberty Ave — Authentic Guyanese Chinese cuisine, great prices, and a welcoming atmosphere.
Tropical Jade Liberty Ave — Guyanese-Chinese fusion. chutney soca playing, doubles on the menu, and a vibe that is entirely its own.
